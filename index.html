What This Is

Generative AI is quickly becoming part of how young people learn, create, and connect. This series brings together researchers, designers, educators, policymakers, and youth-focused practitioners around a shared goal: helping GenAI grow up alongside the kids who use it, safely and thoughtfully.

Who's Behind This

This series is a joint venture among researchers and youth-focused practitioners across several universities. It is co-led by Jake Chanenson and Yaman Yu, with a broader group of collaborators who help shape the workshops, calls for participation, and community around developmentally safe AI for youth.

Jake Chanenson is a CS PhD student at the University of Chicago, where he works with Marshini Chetty in the Amyoli Internet Research Lab. His research sits at the intersection of law, children's privacy, and AI safety for youth, and he also holds a Master of Legal Studies from the University of Chicago Law School. His work has appeared at ACM CHI, ACM FAccT, and PETS. More at jakec007.github.io.

Yaman Yu is an incoming assistant professor at the University of Washington Information School and a 2025 Google PhD Fellow. She earned her PhD in Information Sciences at the University of Illinois, and her research spans usable security and privacy, privacy policies, and generative AI safety for youth. More at her profile.
